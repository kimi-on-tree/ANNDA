1- For sin(2x), approximately 10 units are required to get an absolute residual error below 0.1, 25 units for 0.01, 50 for 0.001.
For the square(2x) function, significantly more units are required, approximately 50 to get an error below 0.1. However, the errors of 
0.01, and 0.001 were not achieved even with larger number of units. Furthermore, when the number of units approaches the number of training samples, 
numerical instability seems to appear, increasing the error dramatically. This reflects overfitting and poor generalization near discontinuities. 

2- We can apply a hard threshold to map the continuous RBF output to ±1. Doing this, the smallest number of units that achieve perfect 
classification is 80. This could be useful for binary classification.
